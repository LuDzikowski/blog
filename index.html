Money Creation & Debt

Political Economy 16 February 2025 Luca Dzikowski ~8 Minutes

Money is so intrinsic to everyday life, that I have never asked myself where it comes from or what it is. Last year, in a Political Economy of Finance module at King’s College, we explored the question, ‘where does new money come from?’ When my lecturer directed this question to the class, we all had a pretty good guess. Of course, money is ‘printed’ by the central bank. Wrong. Well, sort of. Theories of money creation – yes, theories, because really there is no definitive answer to this question – are contested, but today there is consensus that both public (central banks) and private (commercial banks) actors contribute to the creation of new money.
